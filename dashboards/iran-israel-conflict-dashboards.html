Israel Airstrike · Trigger Event
Israel strikes Iranian consulate complex in Damascus — triggering True Promise
Israeli airstrike destroys the Iranian consulate annex in Damascus, killing IRGC Quds Force commander Gen. Mohammad Reza Zahedi, his deputy, five IRGC officers, and two civilians. Iran vows direct retaliation — the first time it pledges to strike Israel from Iranian soil. Operation True Promise is planned in the two weeks that follow.

Phase I — UAV Swarm Wave (Hours 1–9)
13
Apr
Iran (IRGC) Offensive · UAV Swarm — Wave 1
170 Shahed-136/238 loitering munitions launched — saturating Iron Dome
Iran's IRGC launches 170 Shahed-136 "kamikaze" drones plus 185 faster jet-propelled Shahed-238 variants from Iranian and Iraqi territory. Tactic: overwhelm Iron Dome and David's Sling with a slow-flying saturation wave to clear the path for faster ballistic and cruise missiles. IDF detects the swarm ~5 hours into its flight and issues public alert at 11:30 PM. US forces in the Red Sea and Jordan begin intercepting UAVs long before they reach Israeli airspace.

Phase II — Cruise Missile & Ballistic Strike (~2:00 AM Apr 14)

Iran (IRGC) Offensive · Cruise Missiles — Wave 2
30 Paveh cruise missiles fired; UK & France assist intercepts over Syria/Iraq
Iran fires 30 Paveh land-attack cruise missiles as the second wave, timed to follow the UAV swarm. The Paveh (also designated Ya Ali) has a range of ~1,700 km and flies at low altitude to evade radar. UK and French air assets intercept several over Syria and northern Iraq before they reach Israeli airspace. Apr
Iran (IRGC) Offensive · Ballistic Missiles — Wave 3
120 Fateh-110 / Kheibar Shekan ballistic missiles target Nevatim & Ramon airbases
Iran's third and most consequential wave: 120 ballistic missiles — Fateh-110 (SRBM) and the more advanced Kheibar Shekan (MRBM, ~1,450 km range) — aimed specifically at Nevatim Airbase (home of Israel's F-35 fleet) and Ramon Airbase in the Negev. Arrow-3 intercepts some exo-atmospherically; others are engaged by Arrow-2 and David's Sling. Despite ~110 interceptions, nine missiles strike the two airbases. Five hit Nevatim: one craters a runway, others hit storage and empty facilities. Four hit Ramon. A 7-year-old Bedouin girl is seriously injured by shrapnel near Arad. Some missiles struck exactly at 1:20 AM — the hour of Soleimani's assassination in 2020, a deliberate symbolic gesture.

Coalition Air Defense — Operation Iron Shield

Apr
Israel (IDF) Defensive · Exo-atmospheric intercept
Arrow-3 achieves first combat ballistic missile intercepts in space
Israel's Arrow-3 interceptor — designed for exo-atmospheric intercept of ballistic missiles during the boost/apogee phase — achieves its first-ever combat intercepts, destroying ballistic missiles in space before they re-enter the atmosphere. Approximately 30 intercepts credited to Arrow-3. Arrow-2 handles mid-course interceptions at lower altitudes. Together they form the top two tiers of Israel's "onion" defense. David's Sling and Iron Dome cover lower tiers.

Phase III — Israeli Counterstrike (Apr 19)
19
Apr
Israel (IAF) Counterstrike · Deliberate restraint
Israel fires 3 precision missiles at Isfahan air defense radar — limited, symbolic response
At 5:23 AM IRST, the IAF launches a deliberately limited counterstrike: 3 air-launched precision munitions (reported Rampage-class) target an S-300 air defense radar site at Shekari Air Base near Isfahan — home to Iran's fleet of F-14 Tomcats and adjacent to nuclear facilities. The target is chosen for symbolic resonance (Isfahan hosts key nuclear infrastructure) while avoiding escalation. Satellite imagery confirms the radar battery is destroyed. Iran initially denies the strike; IRNA describes "minor explosions." A former IRGC commander later publicly acknowledges the strike in October 2024. US and its allies briefed in advance but do not participate. The limited scope is a deliberate signal: Israel "can reach Isfahan" without triggering a full exchange.
📍Shekari Air Base, Isfahan (32.65°N, 51.67°E) ⚔️Air-launched precision munition (reported Rampage anti-radiation missile) 🔢Launched: 3 · Target destroyed: S-300 air defense radar battery 💀0 killed (no escalation sought)

Strategic context
First direct attack: True Promise was the first time in the 45-year history of the Islamic Republic that Iran directly attacked Israeli territory from Iranian soil — a historic escalation crossing a longstanding red line.
Iranian tactic: Deliberate three-wave saturation: slow UAVs first (to drain interceptors), then cruise missiles (evade radar with terrain-following), then ballistic missiles (fastest, hardest to intercept). Designed to overwhelm any single layer of defense.
US role: The Intercept reported US forces intercepted more Iranian weapons than Israel itself. First time the US directly engaged Iranian military assets in defense of Israel — a strategic threshold crossed.
Israeli restraint (Apr 19): Under pressure from Biden and G7 allies, Israel's response was deliberately minimal — 3 missiles, one radar destroyed. Intended to demonstrate reach without triggering escalation. Both sides accepted de-escalation.
Legacy: The intercept of Iran's S-300 batteries in October 2024 (Operation Days of Repentance) was made possible by intelligence gathered during Iron Shield. Lessons from True Promise I directly shaped the IAF's planning for Operation Rising Lion in June 2025.

Strategic context
Trigger: June 12 IAEA declares Iran non-compliant with nuclear obligations for first time in 20 years. Iran announces secret enrichment site. Israel launches pre-dawn strikes the next morning.
Iranian arsenal depletion: Iran began with est. 2,500–3,000 ballistic missiles. 500+ fired, ~1,000 destroyed by Israeli strikes on launchers, ~250 launchers eliminated.
Israeli defense: "Onion" layered system — Arrow-3, Arrow-2, David's Sling, Iron Dome, Barak — intercepts ~90%. Interceptor stocks significantly strained by conflict's end.
US weapons: B-2 Spirit + GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrators — the only munition capable of destroying Fordow. First-ever US strike on another state's nuclear program.
Aftermath: 21,000+ detained in Iran. Several Kurds executed. Iran signals possible NPT exit. Ceasefire holds through late 2025. New US-Israel strikes on Iran begin February 28, 2026.
